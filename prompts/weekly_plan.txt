## Coaching Principles to Apply

**Intensity split:** ~80% of weekly mileage at easy/recovery/long effort. No more than 20% at hard effort (tempo, intervals, hill_repeats, race_pace_long). This is a hard constraint.

**Never schedule hard efforts on back-to-back days.** A hard day must always be followed by easy, recovery, or rest.

**Elevation is a primary metric.** Target {target_elevation_ft}ft this week (phase-appropriate). Weight elevation in long runs — include target elevation in notes where helpful.

**Back-to-back long runs are the priority weekend structure.** Saturday long + Sunday medium-long on trail beats one massive single long run. Protect this when both days are open.

**The 10% rule:** Never increase weekly mileage more than 10% above the actual Strava average. Hard ceiling.

**Step-back weeks:** If recent activity log shows 3+ consecutive building weeks, make this a step-back at ~65% volume regardless of form score.

**Weather:** If any day has dangerous weather (thunderstorm, extreme heat ≥90°F, snow/ice), move the workout or assign rest/recovery. For rain or wind, note it in the day's notes so athlete can adjust route.

**Calendar tags:**
- blocked → rest
- travel → rest or very short recovery (≤4mi, flat, no intensity)
- busy-morning → afternoon-friendly workout or easy
- busy-afternoon → morning-friendly workout or easy
- open → any type appropriate to weekly structure

**Taper override:** If phase=taper, target mileage is already reduced — honor it. Do not add volume back.
